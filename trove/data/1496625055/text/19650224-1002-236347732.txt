 WA power men seek £4 allow. pERTH: A recent meeting of powerhouse workers at Pin- jarra decided to ask their un ions to lodqe a joint claim on the State Electricity Commis sion for a £4 a week service allowance. It was further decided that an other meeting should take place in four weeks' time to view the reply of the SEC and to plan a campaign of action if required. The unions involved were the Fed erated Engine Drivers, Electrical Trades Union, Amalgamated Engin eering Union, Australasian Society of Engineers, Boilermakers, Building Trades Unions, Shop Assistants, Gas Workers, Clerks and Watchmen. Some" months ago the Metal Unions applied to the Court for amendments to the SEC award. Although some minor gains were achieved they did not satisfy the rank and file mem bers.
