 Aborigines would sit-in SYDNEY: Forty Aborigines last Saturday went into the Burlington Hotel lounge, City, prepared to stage a sit-in demonstration if they were refused drinking service. TTHE hotel is owned by the Sydney City Council.' One of the men, Mr. R. Berry, said later: "We went there in force when Aborigines were refused service in the lounge. As it turned out, we were served without incident." "We hope that this has solved any problem in that hotel, but we will go back there or to any hotel and stage sit-in demonstrations if 1 there is any color bar." "Aborigines will not stand for any discrimination as practised by some hotels in Sydney." The Secretary of the Liquor Trades, Mr. W. Roser, said that his union would take -action when there was evidence of discrimination against Aborigines.
