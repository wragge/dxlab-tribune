 Messages of solidarity SYDNEY: Messages of greetings have been sent by trade union leaders in Melbourne and Sydney to the In ternational Trade Union Committee of Solidarity with Workers and People of South Vietnam. The conference is due to open this week at Hanoi in the Democratic Rep ublic of Vietnam. Mr. R. Rose (senior vice president, Newcastle Branch of the .Waterside Workers' Federation) was to have at tended the conference on behalf of the Federation's Federal Council, but was refused a visa by the Menzies Government. A joint message sent by over 50 trade Union leaders in Melbourne said they opposed "the policy of the Aus tralian Government sending Austra lian troops to Vietnam in support of United States aggressors, and pledge to actively campaign for the with drawal of all Australian and American military personnel from Vietnam." Several unions in Sydney have also sent cables supporting the conference.
