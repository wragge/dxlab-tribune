 Conscription— 1916 and now THE fiftieth anniversary of the great defeat of W. M. Hughes' attempt to introduce conscription for overseas ser- vice in the first referendum on the issue in 1916 is to he commemorated by a special meeting sponsored by the re cently formed Anti-Conscrip tion Committee in Victoria. Principal speaker at the meeting, to he held at the Richmond Town Hall on Saturday, October 23, at 8 pm, will be Federal Opposi tion leader A. A. Calwell. Tribune will be commem orating the anniversary — which falls on October 28 — with a special article by an active participant in the anti-conscription fight. We invite readers who may also have been involved in the referendum campaign to send us reminiscences of the struggle.
